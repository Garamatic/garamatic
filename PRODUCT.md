# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Organizations and teams that need workflow automation and dispatch — incident response teams, infrastructure operators, and support desks. They are practical, busy, and need a tool that gets out of their way. The product serves multi-tenant deployments where each tenant (Whitman, Liberty, Hennessey, Desgoffe) has distinct operational needs.

## Product Purpose

Ticket Masala is a malleable software platform for workflow automation and dispatch. It routes, prioritizes, and resolves operational workflows across tenants — from rail infrastructure incident reporting to generalized ticket management. The platform is built to be shaped to each customer's domain, not force them into a fixed model.

## Brand Personality

Quirky, upstart, different, small is beautiful. Not a corporate behemoth — the scrappy challenger that out-engineers the incumbents. The name "Masala" itself signals a blend of influences, a mix of spice and substance. The brand carries personality in copy and details, but respects that the user is there to get work done.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate SaaS cliché**: No generic blue navbars, cookie-cutter dashboards, stock photography, or Bootstrap defaults. No "enterprise-grade" bloat.
- **AI hype aesthetic**: No purple gradient badges, brain illustrations, or chatbot-first UIs. AI (GERDA) is a tool, not a personality.

## Design Principles

1. **Transparency** — Users always know what's happening. Status is visible, system feedback is clear, state is never ambiguous.
2. **Speed** — Every interaction feels instant. Latency is a design problem, not just an engineering one. Optimistic UI, skeleton screens, minimal page weight.
3. **Delight** — Unexpected polish in the details. Thoughtful microcopy, satisfying micro-interactions, animation that communicates not decorates.
4. **Consistency** — Patterns repeat predictably. A button in one view works the same in another. The mental model compounds.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Skip links, visible focus states, sufficient color contrast, screen reader support, reduced motion support. The product must work for all operators regardless of ability.
